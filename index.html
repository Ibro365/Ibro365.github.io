My Biography

My name is Ibrahim Moallim-Ahmed. I am 21 years old and I was born and raised in Toronto. I live with both of my parents and 2 of my brothers. I am the 2nd eldest. I am a college student in my last semester in the T127 Computer Programmer Analyst Program in George Brown College. I first got into programming when I was in the 10th grade at my high school known as Riverdale Colliegiate Institute. At first i was not very interested and I only took the programming course to get an extra credit. However, as I remained in the course and increased my knowledge and seen myself improve and get better, I started to gain an interest and passion for programming. Unfortunatley, that was my only experience with programming in high school due to me not pursuing it further in grade 11 and 12. During the time when I had to decide what I wanted to do after high school, I was presented with many options and at first I decided to apply to a fire fighter program at Seneca because that was also a field I was interested in at the time. However, it did not work out as I just did not see any future in it for me due to how competitve the field is and the lack of jobs at the time. After that process was over my mother recommended that i apply to a computer programmer program and that's when I remembered how I used to enjoy programming in grade 10 and I had some experience with it. That was when I decided to apply to the Computer Programmer Analyst program at George Brown. Thank you for taking the time to read!
